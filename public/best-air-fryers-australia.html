The Ultimate Australian Buyer's Guide

Capacity

When choosing, consider your household size. Single individuals or couples might find a 4-6L model adequate, while families or entertainers should consider larger 10L+ units.

Cooking Functions

Versatility is key. Models offering multiple functions like grilling, roasting, and baking provide more value.

Footprint and Design

Small kitchens benefit from compact models or those with a slim footprint. All-in-one designs can save space without sacrificing functionality.

Health & Safety

Opt for models that are PFAS-free and have features like auto-shutdown for added safety.

Ease of Cleaning

Look for dishwasher-safe components to minimize post-cooking cleanup. Models with removable baskets and trays add convenience.

Technology

Advanced features like touchscreen controls or app connectivity provide a seamless and interactive cooking experience.

Specific Needs

Adjustable models cater to varied preferences. Busy professionals may seek rapid cooking times, while families may prioritise capacity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do air fryers use a lot of electricity? Air fryers are generally energy-efficient due to their rapid cooking technology. They use less electricity compared to traditional ovens.

What size is best for a family of four? For a family of four, consider a model with at least a 6-8L capacity to accommodate larger meal portions.

Can air fryers cook frozen food? Yes, most air fryers are excellent for cooking frozen foods, offering crispy results without the need for defrosting.

Are air fryers safe for everyday use? Absolutely. They feature safety mechanisms like automatic shut-off and cool-touch exteriors, making them safe for daily cooking.
