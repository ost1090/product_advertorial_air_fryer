The Ultimate Australian Buyer's Guide

Choosing the right air fryer involves assessing several factors:

  1. Capacity: Consider family size and meal proportions. Smaller households may thrive with 4-5L fryers, while large families will benefit from 7-10L options.

  2. Functionality: Many air fryers offer multi-cooking functions such as grilling, roasting, and baking. Opt for multi-functional designs if space is a premium.

  3. Materials: Prioritise models with toxin-free coatings such as ceramic or glass for healthier cooking.

  4. Ease of Use and Cleaning: Look for products with intuitive interfaces and dishwasher-safe components to simplify cleaning.

  5. Special Features: Consider advanced features such as app connectivity or dual zone technology for increased convenience.

Overall, align the choice with your cooking habits, kitchen size, and whether you prioritise technology or manual control.
