The Ultimate Australian Buyer's Guide

Choosing the right air fryer depends on your household's needs, kitchen space, and cooking preferences. Here are key features to consider:

  • Capacity: Smaller fryers (3-4 litres) suit singles or couples, while larger models (over 7 litres) are better for families.
  • Functions: Look for models with multiple functions if versatility is crucial.
  • Material: Prefer models with BPA-free or glass pots for safe, chemical-free cooking.
  • Ease of Cleaning: Dishwasher-safe parts save time and effort.
  • Space: Ensure the fryer fits your kitchen workspace.

For couples and singles living in apartments, a compact model like the Ninja Air Fryer 4.7L Pro might be ideal. Large families or hosts should consider the Ninja XXXL FlexDrawer for its substantial capacity and flexible cooking.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best air fryer size for a family?

For families, aim for at least a 5-6 litre capacity to cook enough food for everyone.

Can I cook multiple dishes at once?

Yes, models like the Ninja Foodi Dual Zone offer dual baskets for simultaneous dish preparation.

How do I clean my air fryer?

Choose a model with dishwasher-safe components for hassle-free cleaning.

Is air frying healthier than traditional frying?

Air frying uses significantly less oil, making it a healthier cooking method.

Does a glass air fryer work better than traditional designs?

A glass pot offers safer cooking by avoiding toxins found in some non-stick coatings.

What features should I prioritise?

Prioritise capacity, cleaning ease, and cooking versatility based on your needs.

Are there air fryers with glass windows for monitoring?

Yes, the Glass Air Fryer provides visibility for perfect cooking control.

What's the advantage of ceramic-coated air fryers?

Ceramic coatings are non-stick and free from harmful chemicals.

Can air fryers be used for baking and grilling?

Most versatile models offer multiple functions, including baking and grilling.

Do larger air fryers cost more to run?

While larger models can consume more energy, they are often more economical due to faster cooking times.
