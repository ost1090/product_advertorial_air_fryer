The Ultimate Australian Buyer's Guide

Choosing the perfect air fryer involves several considerations to match your lifestyle and cooking habits. Here are key factors to keep in mind:

Capacity

Assess the required capacity based on household size. Larger models (9-12L) cater to families or those who enjoy hosting, allowing multiple meals to be cooked simultaneously. Smaller families or singles might opt for models between 3-5L for efficiency and energy savings.

Functions and Features

Look for models offering multiple cooking functions, such as roasting, baking, grilling, and dehydrating, to maximise use. Features like dual baskets or sync cooking can dramatically change how you prepare meals, offering more flexibility and time-saving benefits.

Technology

Advanced air circulation and digital interfaces enhance cooking results and user experiences. Consider models with rapid air technology for even cooking and digital controls for precise temperature and time settings.

Footprint

Evaluate the available space in your kitchen. Compact designs with a smaller footprint are ideal for limited bench space, while larger models may require more room.

Safety and Maintenance

Prioritise models that are dishwasher safe for easy cleaning. PFAS-free models offer a healthier cooking experience, especially important for conscious consumers.

Energy Use

Energy-efficient options reduce power consumption without sacrificing performance, an essential consideration for environmentally friendly households.

Final Thoughts

Match the product to your cooking style and household needs to ensure you choose an air fryer that becomes an indispensable kitchen asset.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do air fryers work?

Air fryers circulate hot air around the food to cook it, mimicking the effects of frying but with significantly less oil. This method creates a crispy exterior while keeping the interior tender.

Are all air fryer parts dishwasher safe?

Not all parts are dishwasher safe. Always check the manufacturer's instructions, as many models have removable baskets and trays that can be placed in the dishwasher, simplifying clean-up.

Can I cook frozen foods in an air fryer?

Yes, most air fryers are excellent at cooking frozen foods quickly and efficiently, delivering crispy results without needing defrosting.

What size air fryer should I buy?

It depends on your household size and cooking needs. Larger families might opt for models over 7L, while singles or couples may find 3-5L units adequate.

Is using an air fryer healthier than traditional frying?

Air fryers require less oil, reducing the fat and calories typically consumed in fried foods, making it a healthier alternative.

Can I use foil or parchment in an air fryer?

Yes, both foil and parchment paper are generally safe to use but ensure they do not block air circulation.

How energy-efficient are air fryers?

Air fryers are usually more energy-efficient than traditional ovens, cooking food faster and consuming less electricity.

Do air fryers dry out food?

When used correctly, air fryers maintain food moisture while creating a crispy outer layer, though overcooking can lead to dryness.

Can I bake a cake in an air fryer?

Yes, many air fryers have baking functions, allowing you to make cakes and other baked goods. However, results can vary based on the model.

What dishes are ideal for air frying?

Air fryers are versatile, perfect for making chips, chicken wings, roasted vegetables, baked goods, and even reheating leftovers with better texture than a microwave.
